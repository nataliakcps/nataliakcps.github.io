Pierogi

One of Poland's most famous dishes. The Slavic word pir means 'a feast'. First recorded evidence was in the 17th century. This evidence stated that in the 13th century Saint Jacek Odrowąż fed theses dumplings to the poor in Kraków during the Tatar invasion. They uses to be made on special occasions but today they are eaten everyday. There are many types of pierogi:uszka,pierożki,etc. Pierogi ruskie are stuffed with mashed patatoes and quark cheese. There are also ones with meat filling like:pork,chicken and beef.

Visit culture.pl

Description of Receipe

Ingredients

  • Butter
  • Flour
  • Egg
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Cheddar
  • Potatoes

Directions

Cheesey Potato Filling

  1. Boil potato in salted water,start with them in cold water
  2. Drain potato
  3. Mash potato and then add the cheese,butter,salt and pepper
  4. Spread out onto a tray and let it cool on counter for 30 min then in refrigerator for 1 hour

Pierogi Dough

  1. Whisk flour and salt into a mixing bowl
  2. Make a well in the center and then add melted butter,warm water,and egg. Then mix with spatula
  3. Knead the dough for 5 min on a surface with a bit of flour
  4. Let the Dough rest for 30 min

Making the Pierogi

  1. Roll out dough on floured surface to 3mm thick
  2. Use a glass cup to cut out the circles in the dough
  3. Put 1 tablespoon of filling into center of the circle dough
  4. Dip finger in water and put around the edge of half of the circle
  5. Fold dough and press the edges to seal it, then pinch around the edges to seal it tightly
  6. Use a pan and boil the water to cook them. Cook for 8-10 min
